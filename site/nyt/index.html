Activities:

Here are some other things I’ve been up to:

I use Are.na to write, order, and discuss my ideas with other people. Last spring, I had the chance to present one of my channels at an are.na event and write about it for their blog.

Goby is my ongoing personal project to create a new interface for organizing and representing information. You can find my documentation here, and feel free to reach out! I love talking to people thinking about how interfaces shape our thought.

This past June I developed a web interactive for L.A. Times “Behold” portrait project, designed by the amazing Alex Tatusian.

Tractatus Logico-Philosophicus Reader visit

This project presents a single text, Wittgenstein’s Tractatus, in 4 different ways, experimenting with different forms of reading and navigation.

The distress signal

Narrative told through descending levels of an isometric drawing (written and illustrated by me).

To say a name (in)correctly

Experimental essay discussing the ethical implications of pronouncing someone’s name incorrectly.

Theory and practice visit

Two interlinked essays presented side-by-side in an experimental web reader. I wrote them during my Parsons thesis, reflecting on the making process and the philosophy driving it.

Goby — Parsons thesis info

Desktop app for organizing files and ideas, which allows you to alternate between table and graph representations of the same information.

Words and Pictures

Two-sided risograph booklet comparing the qualities of expression through language versus images (written/illustrated by me).

“Behold” project (development only) visit

Interactive photo piece on Black culture in Los Angeles, built as a freelance developer working closely with designer Alex Tatusian.

Afghan women under the Taliban visit

Custom build for an L.A. Times profile of Afghan women facing the return of the Taliban. Built in collaboration with foreign correspondent Marcus Yam and photo editor Kate Kuo.

“The Secret” website visit

Animated opening for an imaginary Shakespeare theatre company website, making use of a passage from King Lear.

What’s on your plate? visit

Interactive for a story showcasing people around L.A. with interesting vanity plates. Built in collaboration with a team of reporters and photographers while interning at The L.A. Times.

Sonnet index visit

Shakespeare’s 154 sonnets, presented as a single block of unbroken text. Visitors can browse each sonnet and learn about the body of work through filtering and editing features.
